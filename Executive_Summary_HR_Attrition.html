Project Overview
The HR analytics team sought to develop a supervised classification model to predict which employees are at risk of leaving the company, using satisfaction, performance, workload, tenure, and compensation data. The model was built using a Random Forest Classifier to capture non-linear relationships between employee experience and attrition.
Key Insights
  • ● Satisfaction level is the single strongest driver of attrition — employees who leave report markedly lower satisfaction scores than those who stay.
  • ● Workload extremes matter: employees with very high project counts and monthly hours, or very few, are more likely to leave, pointing to burnout and disengagement as twin risks.
  • ● Tenure plays a role: attrition risk rises around the 4–6 year mark, suggesting a "plateau point" where employees reassess their career path.
  • ● Lower salary bands and lack of recent promotion are linked to higher attrition, giving HR clear, actionable levers (pay review, promotion pathways) to target retention.
Details
The team built a Random Forest Classifier (300 trees) to predict attrition using 9 structural and engagement features, after one-hot encoding department and ordinally encoding salary band.

The model achieves 99.1% accuracy and an AUC of 0.99 on the held-out test set, with precision of 99.6% and recall of 96.6% for identifying employees who left — meaning very few at-risk employees are missed and very few false alarms are raised.

A simpler Logistic Regression baseline reached only 79.6% accuracy and an AUC of 0.83, confirming that attrition risk is driven by non-linear interactions (e.g., satisfaction combined with workload) that linear models cannot fully capture.
Next Steps
Validate before deployment: Confirm no data leakage and test the model on a future, out-of-time cohort given the unusually high accuracy.
Operationalize risk scores: Build a dashboard ranking employees by predicted attrition probability for proactive HR outreach.
Enrich data: Add finer-grained compensation, engagement survey, and manager-relationship data to improve precision further.
Monitor fairness: Audit predictions across departments and salary bands to ensure no group is unfairly flagged.
